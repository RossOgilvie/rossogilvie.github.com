23.
Subharmonic Functions

Let Ω ⊂ ℝn be an open and connected region. A continuous function v : Ω¯ → ℝ is called subharmonic if for all x ∈Ω and r > 0 with B(x,r) ⊂Ω it lies below its spherical mean: v(x) ≤𝒮[v](x,r).

(a)
Let v1,v2 be two subharmonic functions. Show that v = max ⁡ (v1,v2) is subharmonic.
(1 point)
(b)
Suppose that v is twice continuous differentiable. Show that v is subharmonic if and only if −Δv ≤ 0 in Ω. (3 points)
(c)
Prove that every subharmonic function obeys the maximum principle: If the maximum of v can be found inside Ω then v is constant. (2 points)
24.
Liouville’s Theorem

Liouville’s theorem (3.10 in the script) says that if u is bounded and harmonic, then u is constant. In this question we give a geometric proof in ℝ2 using ball means defined when B(x,r)¯ ⊂Ω through

M[v](x,r) = 1 ωnrn ∫ B(x,r)v(y)dy.

Theorem 3.5 states that if u is harmonic then it obeys u(x) = M[u](x,r). This beautiful proof comes from the article Nelson, 1961.

(a)
Consider two points a,b in the plane which are distance 2d apart. Now consider two balls, both with radius r > d, centred on the two points respectively. Show that the area of the intersection is (2 bonus points)
areaB(a,r) ∩ B(b,r) = 2r2acos(dr−1) − 2dr2 − d2
(b)
Suppose that v is a bounded function on the plane: − C ≤ v(x) ≤ C for all x and some constant C. Show that (2 points)
|M[v](a,r) −M[v](b,r)| ≤ 2C ω2 (π − 2acos(dr−1) −2d r 1 − d2 r−2)
(c)
Let u ∈ C2(ℝ2) be a bounded harmonic function. Complete the proof that u is constant.
(2 points)
